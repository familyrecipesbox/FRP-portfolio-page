Project Description

The original idea was first conceived by Mary Bellapu, who wanted to create an app that would store recipes of her grandmother's homecook food she loves and missed. Through the Coding Bootcamp Full Stack Program at University of California, Berkeley, Ryan Mcilvenna and Artawood Chitamitara joined with Mary Bellapu as a group project in working together on designing and developing the Family Receipes Box App.

Project Duration: 2 weeks

Project Completion: July 14, 2018

Project Keywords: App Development, Front-End Development, Back-End Development, Full Stack Development, UX/UI Design, Firebase

Process

In the begining of the project, we started with brainstorming problems users may have when it comes to remembering recipes of their parents or grandparents, and trying to cook the food that they once enjoyed. Then we proceeded with designing the app by exploring user's need, mapping out user's tasks, developing wireframes, and preparing assets for UI development. After finishing the design process, we dive into the development process starting with front-end development to set up HTML and CSS frameworks. Simultaneously, we explored external libraries, APIs, and database frameworks for back-end solutions. Then we sync together the front-end and back-end parts, along with the APIs. In the final part of the development process, we put the app on a local host via node JS for testing with realtime database before deploying the app on a hosting site.

Marketing Analysis

During the brainstorming process, the question we asked ourselves was "how can this app make money?" In doing some marketing analysis, we envision the app to generate revenue from two sources; referral fees from third party suppliers and user generated contents.

Referral Fees

One possible revenue source is referral fees from online meal kit service companies. These companies may include Blue Apron, HelloFresh, Urban Remedy, as examples. The Family Recipes Box App can provide options for the users to buy ingredients from one of these companies. Once a user selects one of these meal kit service companies, the app will send ingredients information based on a user's recipe to the meal kit service company to pack and deliver the ingredients to the users. Some of these companies provide cooking services so that pre-cooked recipes can be delivered to users. Every time a user elects to buy ingredients from any of the meal kit service companies, the app will collect a "referral fee". To expand further, if a user elects to subscribe scheduled repeated deliveries of ingredients or pre-cooked recipes with one of these meal kit service companies, the app will be able to establish steady revenue stream from a percentage of user's subscription fees.

User Generated Contents

Another possible revenue source is from user generated contents (UGC). The Family Recipes Box App is able to collect various non-sensitive user's data, which can include geo-location data, ingredients, food categories, food types, recipe sharing information, and others. Data from UGC can be used to provide useful information to third-parties via API calls. For example, large supermarket chains such as Safeway, Albertons, Kroger, etc. can make API calls to collect information on users' geolocation and the ingredients users buy to improve logistics and marketing strategies so ingredients can be distributed to areas that have high demand, as an example.
